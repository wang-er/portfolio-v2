Book Design.

Two books, a type book and a script.

I have currently made two book designs, one is called 24 Logos & Typefaces and the other is a script book based off of the movie How to Train Your Dragon. Both books were done during my Typography 1 Class.

Scroll down to see each book!


24 Logotypes & Typefaces


24 Logotypes & Typefaces is a 108 page book that features 24 different typefaces. Each typeface includes the designer of the typeface, a brief history about it, a brief combination of two letters from the type, and a foodie picture using a character from the typeface. This book was made with Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, and Indesign.

I chose foodie photos because food is delicious, and there's a lot of variety to make the book seem colorful and cheerful. It was also a great way to practice my photoshop skills by skewing the letters to make them seem realistic.


How to Train Your Dragon


How to Train Your Dragon is a 194 page book that features the entire transcript of DreamWorks' movie How to Train Your Dragon. This script book was done purely through InDesign.

For this book I decided to go with the typeface Serifa. My biggest reason why I chose this typeface because I felt like it represented the movie the most. While the movie takes place in a more classic or ancient-esque tale, the actual movie is for modern kids. I felt like the font couldn't be too traditional and too modern, I needed to find a combination of both. I decided to go with a slab-serif –– It made the book feel classic, but also feel modern at the same time.
