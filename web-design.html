Mobile & Web Mockups.

Mockups related to Web Design.

I've started to work on mockups regarding both web and mobile design. One of these designs, Penny Juice, was done during Interaction Design 1. On the other hand, the Dream Journal app design was done during my free time.

Both of these designs were done through Adobe XD.



Penny Juice


For a project in Interaction Design, we were required to pick a website and redesign it. I decided to go with pennyjuice.com. One of the reasons why I chose it was because the website concept seemed simple, but their company overcomplicated the website. Second off, it's juice. Third off, the history of this company is mildly concerning yet entertaining at the same time.

We had to redesign the home page and one other page. In the document I've included my high fidelity wireframes along with some of my design process prior to designing the final copy.

For this design, I knew it was mainly for people who were working with children. I made the colors seem "fresh", and I made the font a bit more sleek. While the juice is for the children, the site is meant to be catered towards the adults potentially buying the juice.


Dream Journal


Dream Journal was a mockup I decided to make during my freetime. I'm a really big fan of sleep and dreaming, and I've tracking my sleep since 2016. Initially, I was tracking to see if I talked in my sleep. However, I soon began to track my dreams instead. Throughout my time, I've tried out many apps to track my dreams, but there were a lot of issues I had with each of them. First off, most apps I've tried had the purpose of sharing dreams rather than documenting them. During my free time, I decided to make my own dream journal app with the things I wished other apps had included.

One example for an app feature is being able to quickly write/type. It's important that people are able to quickly open their phones and write their dreams down ASAP, because most people forget their dreams 5 minutes after they wake up! In order to counter this, my imaginary phone app has 2 features. First off, it has a quick write mode in the home screen. It takes a huge chunk of the home screen actually. That way you can immediately open up the app and just write. Second off, if that's too slow for you, you can press the lock button. That way, the app hopefully doesn't quit, and as you unlock your phone, the first thing you see is a huge notepad.
